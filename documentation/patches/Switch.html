Switch

A Switch remembers a state. It works just like a light switch — it starts out as off, when you flip it it’ll turn on, and then if you flip it again it’ll turn off. For example, if you wanted to tap a photo and have it zoom in and stay that way when you release your finger, you could hook up the button tap to a Switch which would remember whether the photo should be zoomed in or not.

The Flip port is useful for a symmetrical interaction — e.x. tapping a photo repeatedly to zoom it to large and small sizes. The Turn On and Turn Off port are useful for other interactions where you have different actions to control the switch. For example, for a compose window, you might have the compose button turn on the switch (animating in the window) and the Cancel and Done buttons in the window turning off the switch.

Switch

  • Flip
  • Turn Off
  • Turn On
  • On / Off
Flip

Takes a pulse to flip the state of the switch. If the switch is off this will turn it on, and if it’s on, this will turn it off.

Turn Off

Takes a pulse to turn the switch off. If it’s already off, sending a pulse to this port will have no effect.

Turn On

Takes a pulse to turn the switch on. If it’s already on, sending a pulse to this port will have no effect.

On / Off

Outputs a 0 if the switch is off and a 1 if it’s on.
